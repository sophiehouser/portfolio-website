TAMPON RUN / GIRL CODE

Code, Web and iOS Development, Design, Public Speaking & Author

In 2014, when I was 17, I started to question why I was so uncomfortable talking about my period and the larger implications of that discomfort. In response, a friend and I created a simple web game, Tampon Run, to get people thinking and talking about the stigma around menstruation. It went viral overnight, garnering international press, winning a Webby Award for web games and a Tribeca Disruptive Innovation Award. A few months later, we worked with a team at Pivotal Labs to develop an iOS version of the game and three years later, I co-wrote a book for HarperCollins based on our experiences to encourage girls to learn to code and speak up. It was featured on several best book lists. I was also doing public speaking around the world about these topics.

PUSHING MY COMPUTER TO ITS LIMITS

Code & Design

After my past 18 months of full time software engineering, I’ve come to realize that computers, and our relationships with them, are more human than we may think. They exert power over us by keeping us addicted and dependent. We exert power over them by writing the code that lets them run. We need them as portals to the rest of the world. They need us to feed them electricity to function. They overwhelm us with too much information. We do the same back when we open too many tabs, run too many programs. I wanted to visualize this last point: our power to push our computers to their limits. As the number of faces you input increases, so does the choppiness of the animation as the computer loses enough processing power to create a smooth visual.

MEINE PEANUT BRAND

Graphic Design, UI, Web Development

I love peanut butter and I’ve been making it at home throughout the past year. Supermarket peanut butter machines, common in many grocery stores in the US, haven’t become popular in Berlin, and peanut butter here is often expensive and subpar. I decided I’d start selling my own peanut butter and peanut saute sauce. It turned out though that I was more excited to create the website and branding than to sell my product, so my peanut business is on pause (for now).
