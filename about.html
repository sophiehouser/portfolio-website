Sophie Houser

I love new strange situations, whether I find them through the projects I make, technologies I explore or places I go. I think when I, and others, are pushed out of our comfort zones safely, we’re able to try new ways of being, express novel ideas and have an openness to learn from others in ways we don’t in our day to day lives. I’m especially interested in using playfulness and humor as tools to create these experiences.

When I was 17, I co-created a simple video game that featured a girl throwing tampons at oncoming enemies with a preamble about the stigma around menstruation. The game went viral overnight, generating thought and conversation globally around the menstrual taboo. It was a lesson not just in the power of play and humor to get people thinking and talking about something uncomfortable, but also the huge impact a small project can have, especially when the medium is code.

After getting my B.A. at Brown University, where I studied Computer Science and Visual Arts, I moved to Berlin where I’ve worked for the past year and a half as a software engineer at Delivery Hero. I’ve learned there how to build backend services at a massive scale, and I’m now looking to use these skills in a more creative way, as I’d like to get back to my roots in animation, painting, creative coding and more.
